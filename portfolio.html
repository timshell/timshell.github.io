academic research

In academic research settings, your objective/product is to write high-impact research papers. I am finishing up my PhD at Princeton University, where I created two influential programs.

Explainable AI for Human Decision-Making

Our research (published in Science and PNAS) shows how to (a) build state-of-the-art machine learning models of human behavior and (b) probe these models to identify precise and actionable insights.

Sample Implications:
  • Let's build an AI that can predict cancer diagnoses better than a doctor. But, we won't stop there. Then, we 'do science' on these models so that we can identify what the AI is picking up on that the doctor is missing. The doctor can now (i) improve his/her/their own skills and (ii) increase their trust in the AI to make diagnostic decisions.

  • Playing an athlete is predictive of wins, but analysts can't understand why. Our work helps the analyst understand what the model is identifying. Perhaps it reflects a complicated function of their basic stats, or perhaps it reflects some 'intangibles' (e.g. a good teammate) which we can reverse-engineer.

Neuroscience of Cognitive Fatigue + Boredom

Why do we feel tired or bored after working? Our research published in Psychological Review offers a new, rational perspective on why (and when) resting is beneficial. The general idea is that your brain is actually very active at 'rest'. Specifically, hippocampal replay is a brain function that integrates recent memories with your general decision-making system. We can compute the value of this integration process and compare/contrast it with the value of the current task to identify whether it is rational to work or rest at any time point.

Sample Implications:
  • What types of rest are actually restorative? Watching TV may be leisurely, but one often doesn't feel rejuvenated afterwards. Contrast this with walking, which is often rejuvenating and able to facilitate creative insights.

  • Can we enhance the restorative process by stimulating the neural mechanisms responsible for recovery? This would allow us to rest more quickly and efficiently. Can we extend similar ideas to sleep?
